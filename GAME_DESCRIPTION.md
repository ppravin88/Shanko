### How to Play

**The Basics**
- 2-8 players compete across 7 unique rounds
- Each player receives 11 cards at the start of each round
- Form triplets (3 matching cards) and sequences (4 consecutive cards of same suit)
- Draw a card, optionally meld your combinations, then discard
- First player to "go out" ends the round with zero points
- Lowest cumulative score after 7 rounds wins!

**Progressive Challenge System**
Each round has unique winning conditions that increase in complexity:

- **Round 1**: Form 2 triplets (6 cards)
- **Round 2**: Form 1 triplet + 1 sequence (7 cards)
- **Round 3**: Form 2 sequences (8 cards)
- **Round 4**: Form 3 triplets (9 cards)
- **Round 5**: Form 2 triplets + 1 sequence (10 cards)
- **Round 6**: Form 2 sequences + 1 triplet (11 cards)
- **Round 7**: Form 3 sequences (12 cards)

This progression ensures that games stay fresh and challenging from start to finish.

### Unique Strategic Mechanics

**The Buying System**
Don't wait for your turn! When another player discards a card you need, you can "buy" it by placing a card from the draw pile face-down on top of it. This adds both the discarded card and the face-down card to your hand.

Strategic considerations:
- You only get 3 buys per round‚Äîuse them wisely!
- Buying is disabled in 2-player games
- Once you meld, you forfeit remaining buys
- Buy priority goes to players closest to their turn

**Joker Management**
Jokers are powerful wild cards worth 50 points if left in your hand. Use them strategically:
- Substitute for any card in triplets or sequences
- Swap them out later when you get the actual card
- Reuse swapped Jokers to extend sequences
- Note: Jokers can only be swapped from sequences, not triplets!

**Meld Timing**
Deciding when to reveal your combinations is crucial:
- Meld early to minimize risk if someone else goes out
- Wait to surprise opponents by melding and going out in one turn
- Consider opponent progress before committing
- Remember: once you meld, you can't buy anymore!

### Scoring System
Points are calculated based on cards remaining in your hand when someone goes out:
- Number cards (2-10): Face value
- Face cards (J, Q, K): 10 points each
- Aces: 15 points
- Jokers: 50 points

The player who goes out scores zero for that round. After 7 rounds, the player with the lowest cumulative score wins!
